Autobiography of Mahatma Gandhi

Early Life

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.

Education and Career

Gandhi studied law in London and practiced in South Africa, where he developed his political views.

Struggle for Indian Independence

Gandhi returned to India and became a leader in the Indian National Congress, advocating for nonviolent resistance.

Satyagraha and Salt March

He led several movements, including the Salt March, to protest British salt monopoly and unfair laws.

Independence and Assassination

India gained independence in 1947, but Gandhi was assassinated on January 30, 1948, by a Hindu extremist.

"Be the change that you wish to see in the world." - Mahatma Gandhi

Timeline of Events

1869: Mahatma Gandhi born in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.

1893: Gandhi travels to South Africa to work as a lawyer.

1915: Gandhi returns to India and joins the Indian National Congress.

1930: Salt March begins, a major act of civil disobedience against British rule.

1947: India gains independence from British rule.

1948: Mahatma Gandhi assassinated by Nathuram Godse.
